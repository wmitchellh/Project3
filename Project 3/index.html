Tenants in the Chapel Hill's Central Downtown District are Historically More Sucessful

Statistical insight into downtown businesses

By Mitchell Hedrick | @mitchellhedrick | whedrick@live.unc.edu | Oct 22, 2019

Chapel Hill occupies about 20 square miles of Central North Carolina. It is home to one of the oldest public universities in the nation, the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. While the majority of the city limits sprawl northeast of UNC’s campus, the university represents the cultural center Chapel Hill. Franklin Street tenants pay expensive rents in hopes of gaining popularity among students, but the turnover rate for restaurants is famously high.

A dataset assembled by the Town of Chapel Hill shows that the over 25 years old club is quite exclusive among downtown tenants. The majority of businesses are still in their early stages, and have yet to solidify themselves as town staples.

Downtown Chapel Hill is divided into three districts; Central, East, and West. A business in the central district survives for an average of 22.49 years, while those in the east district survive for 18.13 years on average, and west district tenants only keep their doors open for an average of 15.02 years.

The three longest standing tenants in downtown are University Presbyterian Church, University Baptist Church, and First Baptist Church. They are located in the East, Central, and West districts respectively.

This average spatial distribution of tenants is quite different from the evenly spread churches that hold the top three spots. The donut chart below shows that 40.8% of tenants are located in the east district, 45.4% in the west district, and only 13.7% in the central district. While the central district has the smallest number of businesses, they tend to be the most successful.

Restaurant, Retail, and Service are by far the most popular categories of downtown businesses.
